The Hobby of Collecting Clothes

Collecting clothes blends fashion industry, material knowledge and personal identity. From rare denim to artisanal footwear, each piece is a timestamp of design and culture.

Focus areas

  • Archival Japanese avant‑garde (YY, CDG)
  • Artisanal footwear (CCP, Guidi)
  • Workwear & heritage (selvedge denim, pigskin)

Why it matters

Garments are functional art. Understanding pattern‑making, leather tanning, and finishing techniques deepens appreciation and informs the individual consumption of the piece.

Best Times to Hunt

Seasonality

  • Jan–Feb: archive clear‑outs, winter boots
  • May–Jun: spring samples & light outerwear
  • Aug–Sep: denim & early boots / coats for deals

Weekly cadence

Set alerts for Sunday evenings and mid‑week mornings. Set aside one maintenance day (conditioning, brushing, storage organization).

Tip: log purchases and care dates. Good records prevent duplicate silhouettes, resellability, and missed maintenance.

Best Sources & Places

Places I look

  • Local consignment stores and vintage fairs
  • Trusted online marketplaces/resellers and brand outlets (Grailed, Buyee, Instagram)
  • Auctions for rare runs & sample lots

What I verify

  1. Measurements vs. pattern block, untagged size
  2. Material & construction (e.g., object-dyed, stitch type)
  3. Repair potential, longevity and aftercare availability

Origin matters: ask for receipts, factory codes, or dye lot info when possible. It is also important to make sure seller is reputable and trusted.

Sourcing, Care, and Storage

Care kit

  • Horsehair brushes (boots, coats)
  • Distilled water: other sources have the potential to leave mineral stains.
  • Microfiber towel (for application)
  • pH‑balanced leather cleaner + conditioner
  • Cedar shoe trees & breathable bags
  • Sewing machine/kit: repair rips, modify fit, and make it your own

Storage

Use wide hangers for coats, fold knits, and keep leather away from heat, opting to store in a cool, dry place. Track rotations to let leathers rest.

Rule of three: verify, condition, document. It preserves the pieces' value and extends wearability.

Purpose & Philosophy

Personal reasons

Clothes archive how I move through life: seasons, studies, milestones. Each piece earns its place.

Cultural value

Well‑made clothes outlive trends. Collecting rewards craft; not trends that will be gone within weeks. These values support makers to keep rare skills alive, inspiring generations to come.

Collecting teaches patience, material literacy, and stewardship.
